Introduction to Climatic Changes

Climate change refers to long-term changes in the average weather patterns that have been observed on Earth.
Climate change is primarily driven by human activities that release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.
While natural factors can also influence the climate, the current trend of global warming and climate change is largely attributed to human-induced factors.
The key causes of climate change include:

Causes of climatic changes

  1. Burning of Fossil fuels:

    2. Fossil fuels

    Causes of burning fossil fuels:

    • The combustion of fossil fuels (coal, oil, and natural gas) for energy is a major source of greenhouse gas emissions, particularly carbon dioxide (CO2).
      Power plants, industrial facilities, and transportation systems contribute significantly to these emissions.
    • Causes of Deforestation

      • Deforestation

      Causes of Deforestation:

      • The clearing of forests for agriculture, logging, or urban development reduces the number of trees available to absorb CO2 through photosynthesis.
        Forests act as carbon sinks, and their destruction contributes to higher concentrations of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.
      • Causes of industrial processes

        • Industrial processes

        Causes of Industrial processes:

        • Certain industrial activities release potent greenhouse gases, such as methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O).
          For example, agricultural practices and the use of nitrogen-based fertilizers can contribute to nitrous oxide emissions, while livestock produce methane during digestion.
        • Causes of Waste Management

          • Waste Management

          Causes:

          • Improper waste disposal, particularly the decomposition of organic waste in landfills, can produce methane.
            Additionally, certain waste incineration processes release greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Impacts of climate changes

  • Risisng of temperatures

    • Average global temperatures are increasing, leading to more frequent and intense heatwaves.
    This can have direct consequences on human health, agriculture, and ecosystems.
  • Extreme Weather Events

    • Climate change is associated with an increase in the frequency and intensity of extreme weather events, including hurricanes, droughts, floods, wildfires, and storms.
    These events can result in significant damage to infrastructure, displacement of populations, and loss of life.
  • Water scarcity

    • Changes in precipitation patterns and increased evaporation can lead to water scarcity in certain regions.
    This, coupled with the melting of glaciers, affects water availability for agriculture, industry, and human consumption.

Case studies:

The Great Barrier Reef
  • Issue: The Great Barrier Reef, the world's largest coral reef system, has been severely affected by climate change.

  • Impact:Rising sea temperatures have led to coral bleaching, a phenomenon where corals expel symbiotic algae, turning them white.
    Prolonged bleaching weakens the corals and makes them more susceptible to diseases.
  • Consequences:
    Coral bleaching and subsequent die-offs harm the biodiversity of the reef, impacting fish and other marine species that depend on it for survival.
    This, in turn, affects the livelihoods of communities reliant on the reef for tourism and fishing.

Methods to prevent

  • Transition to Renewable resources
    Shift from fossil fuels to renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, hydro, and geothermal power. Invest in research and development to improve the efficiency and affordability of renewable technologies.

  • Renewable Energy Zoning (REZoning) - An interactive, web-based platform that identifies, visualizes, and ranks zones that are most suitable for the development of solar, wind, or offshore wind projects

  • Global Atlas for Renewable Energy - A web platform that allows users to access maps of renewable energy resources for locations worldwide

    • Advantages of Renewable Energy

    • Environmental benefits: Renewable energy is environmentally friendly and can lead to cleaner air and water. It can also reduce greenhouse gas emissions and some types of air pollution.

    • Economic benefits: Renewable energy can lead to substantial long-term savings. The cost of generating electricity from wind dropped 66 percent between 2009 and 2016.

    • Sustainability: Renewable energy sources are sustainable and reusable for a long time. They are mostly naturally occurring and don't deplete like fossil fuels.

    • Job creation: Renewable technologies create lots of jobs.

    • Lower reliance on foreign energy sources: Renewable energy can lower reliance on foreign energy sources.

    • Public health: Transitioning to renewable energy can mitigate health risks from air pollution.
